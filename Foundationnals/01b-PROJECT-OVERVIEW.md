# SAKILI Platform

## Overview

SAKILI is a continental AI ecosystem infrastructure platform headquartered in Mauritius and designed to become the trusted gateway for African AI discovery, collaboration, ecosystem intelligence, and cross-border innovation.

The platform enables AI companies, startups, SMEs, nano businesses, research institutions, innovation hubs, governments, and international partners to discover each other, collaborate, and build trusted ecosystem relationships across Africa.

Unlike transactional marketplaces focused on extraction and commissions, SAKILI is designed as ecosystem coordination infrastructure. Its primary mission is to strengthen visibility, interoperability, and collaboration across African AI ecosystems while remaining affordable, accessible, multilingual, and SME-friendly.

SAKILI combines:
- a continental AI registry,
- an ecosystem discovery engine,
- a collaboration and handshake system,
- ecosystem intelligence,
- event infrastructure,
- and trust/verification systems.

The long-term vision is to establish SAKILI as:
# the operating layer for African AI ecosystems.

---

## Goals

1. Build the largest structured registry of African AI organizations and ecosystems.

2. Facilitate measurable cross-border AI collaborations across all African regions.

3. Position Mauritius as a trusted continental AI coordination hub.

4. Create low-friction infrastructure for African AI SMEs and nano businesses.

5. Develop a trusted ecosystem intelligence platform for governments, investors, and institutions.

6. Achieve representation across all 54 African countries.

7. Build scalable infrastructure capable of supporting future AI ecosystem interoperability.

---

## Core User Flow

1. User discovers SAKILI through search, events, or partnerships.

2. User creates an account using email or OAuth authentication.

3. User creates an organization or ecosystem profile.

4. User selects:
   - AI specializations,
   - sectors,
   - languages,
   - collaboration interests,
   - and ecosystem metadata.

5. Platform verifies organization authenticity.

6. Organization becomes searchable within the discovery platform.

7. Users discover organizations using filters and ecosystem navigation.

8. Users initiate collaboration handshakes and partnership requests.

9. Organizations participate in ecosystem events, networking, and workshops.

10. Platform progressively generates ecosystem intelligence and network effects.

3. Users can discover organizations through filters and search.

4. Organizations can initiate collaboration handshakes.

5. Admins can moderate and verify ecosystem participants.

6. The platform supports mobile-first accessibility across African connectivity conditions.

7. The platform maintains multilingual-ready architecture for future localization.

8. Initial ecosystem events can be published and managed through the platform.

9. The system architecture supports future ecosystem intelligence expansion.

10. The platform demonstrates measurable ecosystem participation growth month-over-month.
